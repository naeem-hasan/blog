What I do when I am stuck

And what you should do

 Posted on January 7, 2018  |  2 minutes (328 words)

Everyone gets stuck. Even the genius you always envied struggles with something. Some are quick to recover. That’s good. Some take time, that’s good too. But to give up? Heck no, that’s not good.

So what do I do when I am stuck? Well, I like to follow a certain procedure.

Know thy problem:

If you don’t know your problem well, that makes it so obvious why you’re stuck. You have to have a good understanding of the problem, and what you’re trying to accomplish. If your code is not working, look for the error message. Try to understand what it says. If you’re trying to solve a physics/math problem, make sure you know the kind of output you’re looking for.

Start over:

Forget everything. Start it again from scratch. It often doesn’t just solve the problem, but also improves the approach as a whole.

Solve in steps:

Do what you’re trying to do in steps. Write down the outputs of each step somewhere. Draw diagrams too? Sure. It really helps a lot.

Don’t get too complicated:

Often(in my case, most of the times) the solution is really easy. Many people tend to overcomplicate stuff and thereby overlook silly mistakes.

Try in different ways:

Why don’t you try a different approach to the problem? If that solves it, you can return to your initial method and find out what went wrong. There are often different ways to break something down. Often better a way than your original one, and often the contrary. Regardless, once solved, you should always try to find out why your initial method didn’t work.

Research, and do similar things:

Look up the internet for similar problems and try to solve them. Write similar codes. Read about the topics in Wikipedia, books etc. Read the official documentations, often your answer lies there.

If your problem is still unsolved, you can always Google! But make sure you learn from your mistakes. Otherwise, you’ll do it again.
